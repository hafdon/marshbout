    # Slippery scales

    Flick begins to sing one of the folksongs she learned at the Dacha, "Many are the weevils in my bread," a quite uplifting ditty (perhaps intened by the volk to compensate for the regrettable eventuality of having lost most of one's grain for the season to an infestation). Citing a "too-blessed-to-be-stressed" mentality, she explains in the heat of battle how there was an almost oppressive perkiiness to some of the other folks around the dacha. The elves don't even have a word for stressed, though, and Flick has to code-switch for that part (just one more reason it was hard to grow up there).

    As Jean-Luc says "Let's just pull up here and wait," Flick shouts "Onwards!" and continues the charge. The adventurers huddle in the middle of Dorf's back to avoid the treacherous swarms. Flick sticks her foot out to prevent Jean-Luc from sliding off a milk-slicked scale, and (the rogue now having solid footing enough to scurry up towards the dorsal ridge) she pirouettes over Jean-Luc's head and lands uphill of him. Jaap apologies, "Sorry, sorry," for being the likely cause of the slippery scale.

    Flick, percipient of the terrain's dangers, brings Dorf around in a wide arc to avoid the densest part of the swarms of corrupted creatures. A not-insignificant number of Dorf's scales are shed into the wind like shingles during a hurricane as thick streams of the bugs dig under the scales and begin gnawing at her exposed tissues. The celestial dragon isn't halted in her forward progress, though, and Flick adopts a stable position, and, as has become her trademark, lands successful (and successive) arrows into the poorly armored cerebellum. The gelatinous mass jiggles, sheds tumor-like globules, and begins to drip a black ichor dotted with bright-pink blobs, tufts of tentacles hanging limply.

    Flick gags slightly at the inky leakage as she makes her way around the right-hand side of the Brain.
