    # §080. Maybe after a good swatting

  • §080. I don't want to have to adopt that as the official motto of this campaign

  • §080. Fart Dimension!

    • hogdog preroll

    We left our intrepid adventurers last session just as Flick had exploded Gargan's body in a manner unacceptable to most moral systems ("It's not my fault I do so much damage."), grabbed the grilling fork / wand (which she'd been told was a necessary instrument for providing Gargan's family with grilled meats during their recurring barbeque party ("Gotta have it.")) with her bare hands in order to stuff it into the bag of holding, despite the implicit warning from Jean-Luc.

    She remained facing away from the party for a moment, and nothing odd occurred, but as she turned around, each of her adventuring friends had been replaced by a humanoid-sized insect of the type they fought in Pilzzt's house, each one throwing off clouds of dust and dripping a semi-coagulated, oily ichor. Somehow each one of these squicky monsters provided Flick with an unexpected sense of comradery; -- all except the one in the position until recently occupied by Jean-Luc, wherein appeared a metallic sphere about five feet across radiating a pink-white light and kept aloft by six large full-feathered swan wings, and onto which Flick's ire, and the apparent jeers of the insects, were directed.

    # recap

    Flick, still gripped by a hallucinatory madness, continues to unknowingly fighter friends; her adversary always taking the form of a body-swapping seraphim who has it out for her. Drav notices Ojorg giving off some odd heat, an impression which is reinforced later as he coughs up fire while recovering from spellcasting. Flick indulges her inner metaphor and goes invisible. Jaap tries his hand at diplomacy, but the psychological distance between his voice and the ranger's mind is too great. Drav temporarily traps Flick in an invisible Wall of Force bubble, and Ojorg further aggravates the hermeticized[1] elf by filling the upside-down bowl with noxious gas. In a moment of insight, Ojorg reproduces Drav's voice through Minor Illusion to calm Flick down, and the elf finally triumphs over the madness, though not before realizing that her feelings of friendship with the insects might be more than mere coincidence.

    Jaap notices an odd shadow in a moving light-sculpture by means of which Ojorg is recreating the battle. Though this deepens the gnome's mistrust of the newcomer, Pjorg takes the opposite approach and attempts (unsuccessfully) to begin to learn the art of light-sculpting. And as though lazing around after a fight weren't exciting enough, a transplanar crocodile giant shows up to demand it be given Flick.

    # Expertly and artfully using a chalkboard

    At this, Pjorg turns back to his chalkboard. Given the unassuming size of the object and his blocky handwriting, he's only able to inscribe a handful of runes on it at once with the chalk. Even with his experimentation so restricted, though, he finds that as he sketches out the symbols he's experiencing possible alternatives for ways to extend what he's written, including runes he has no experience with. He draws the first half of the Cloud Rune. (Typically, when he invokes this rune, it feels like when you're grappling with a hard problem, and the multiple things you know about it coalesce and you know the answer in a kind of Eureka moment.) This same sudden feeling manifests in his hand as he draws out the rune, but it seems as though it's been done without the effort it usually takes to get to that spark of insight. Pjorg makes a note of all this.

    # Suspicions

    — I guess with the Library … gone … we should head to Pilzzt's house with the knowledge we have? I'm guessing the sharks are still there.

    — It's hard to tell definitively. I'm pretty sure they weren't part of that … thing. And I may have oversold her before; she is, or was, more like a clerk than a librarian. So I'm not sure where she is now: maybe she's at another library, or Glob forbid was consumed by that creature.

    Flick's having a hard time piecing together where to even backtrack to. Sia's been one bizarre aberration after another, and it's not clear to her that Startrella has their best interests at heart. In fact, maybe she's been leading them into all these traps. As a result, she's disinclined to go along with the starchild's suggestions and perhaps displays that inner intractability and resistance on her face.

    Jaap sticks a suspicious eye on Ojorg, and sees that once the dwarf has gotten back his breath the ignited gasps cease. Otherwise no demonstrable signs of abnormality appear.

    Until, that is, he adopts the posture of some of the artists in the polariton and light-sculpts. The gang recognizes his expertise immediately as he recreates the scene which just took place, starting from Flick's unthinking grasp of the wand, through the battle, with each of the participants depicted. And it looks more like a calming doodling for him.

    He's not attempting to hide anything, but what they'd thought was a smudge, or the different palette he's using (they can't place how, but his process looks qualitatively different from the starchildren they saw) , capturing a blurry image on the furthest edges of the flickering sculpture, a portentous bulk hovering as a satellite.

    It raises the hackles of Jaap and Pjorg; clearly Ojorg has artistic, perhaps subconscious access to whatever this is, invisible to the naked eye.

    — Does anyone have any idea what this is? Jaap, approaching the sculpture, draws their attention to the miniature shadow.     — I'm sorry, what are you pointing at?

    — Sometimes there are echoes, "artifacts"[5], "latents"[6], or "accidents"[7] with me, which I like to attribute to my exotic origin compared to the starchildren. Sometimes spots or blurs will appear, especially if it's behind me, since I have to keep most of the sculpture in the "signing space" (if you've ever used YSL[8])

    Jaap is even more mistrustful now that this one point, which was quite eerie, would disappear, and that there were none other of these blurs in the sculpture. The gnome feels like Ojorg is probably telling the truth; there's a slim chance that he knows what they might be talking about, but he was actually surprised to hear that something appeared behind him.

    Drav is waiting until her friends make a little distance from Ojorg before she tells them about the warm spot on his back.

    # Deblurring

    At that moment, they all detect a powerful electronic burst coming from behind the library in the equivalent position of the light-sculpture blur. In that space they see a creature …

    — I did it! Pjorg cries.

    … the likes of which they've never encountered. About twice as tall as a human, it resembles a bulky mantis with the head of an alligator, harsh blue light streaming from between every exoskeletal plate. A crest of foot-long spines runs along its back from its head to its pelvis, and two thick arms from which emerge apparently random protuberances of bone or gristle, all dripping with some viscous alien fluid. As they stand there, it says, in a deep rumble, — Give me the Walker, and stares at Flick.
